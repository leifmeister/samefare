How trip costs are calculated

SameFare is a cost-sharing platform. Drivers set a price at or below a cap we calculate from real running costs — no profit allowed.

{# ── Step 2: per-seat cap ───────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Step 2 — Per-seat cap

The driver and each passenger share the total trip cost equally — one share per seat plus one share for the driver.

total_cost = distance_km × allowed_cost_per_km
price_per_seat_cap = floor(total_cost / (seats + 1) / {{ policy.rounding_unit if policy else 50 }}) × {{ policy.rounding_unit if policy else 50 }}

The cap is always rounded down to the nearest {{ policy.rounding_unit if policy else 50 }} ISK — never up. Drivers set their own price at or below this cap.

{# ── Step 3: fuel price ─────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Step 3 — Fuel price source

We fetch the 80th-percentile petrol price in Iceland daily from apis.is. If the live feed is unavailable we fall back to a cached value, then to a conservative policy default. The price tier used is recorded in every trip's cost snapshot so calculations are always reproducible.

{# ── Why cost-sharing ───────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Why cost-sharing, not pricing?

Icelandic law distinguishes between commercial transport and genuine cost-sharing. SameFare enforces the cap so drivers recover real expenses without generating profit, keeping the platform legal and fair for everyone.
